Introduction

Can you think of a dish more iconic, adored, or heart-warming than a spaghetti bolognese?

This recipe is going to tick all the boxes: it's simple, quick, and authentic. You'll enjoy a nice thick sauce, a healthy dose of basil goodness, and a lovely seasoning that will make you think you're in the middle of Rome. Pair it with a salad and perhaps even a limoncello, and you've got yourself an Italian feast.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 50g Pancetta, thinly sliced
  • 1 large brown onion, coarsely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 2 tsp spaghetti bolognese seasoning
  • 500g beef mince
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stick, finely chopped
  • 400g can diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups (500ml) beef stock
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 500g barilla spaghetti
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Basil leaves, to serve
  • Parmesan cheese, to serve

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large heavy-based saucepan over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook for 2 mins. Add the onion and garlic. Cook for 2 mins or until the onion starts to soften. Increase heat to high. Add the mince. Cook, stirring with a wooden spoon to break up lumps, for 5 mins or until browned.

  2. Stir in the bolognese seasoning, carrot and celery. Cook, stirring, for 3 mins. Add diced tomatoes, beef stock and bay leaves. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 20 mins or until sauce reduces and thickens slightly. Salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Meanwhile, cook spaghetti in a saucepan of boiling water following packet directions. Drain.

  4. Divide spaghetti among serving bowls. Spoon over the sauce. Top with basil and sprinkle with parmesan.
